FIFA Men's World Rankings (End of 2022)

As of 2022, the FIFA Men's World Rankings and the FIFA Women's World Rankings are key references for national football teams' standings globally. Below are the top-ranked teams in both categories according to the end-of-year rankings for 2022. Keep in mind that these rankings are subject to change based on international matches, tournaments, and other factors influencing the teams' points.

Highlighting some key points from the FIFA Men's World Rankings at the end of 2022:

  • Brazil Topped the Rankings: Brazil secured the top spot in the FIFA Men's World Rankings by the end of 2022. This was a testament to their strong performances in international fixtures, including the CONMEBOL World Cup Qualifiers and their exhibitions in friendlies and tournaments throughout the year.
  • Croatia's Consistency: Croatia, the 2018 World Cup runners-up, showcased their ability to remain competitive on the world stage, maintaining a spot in the top 10 despite the small size of the country and its football league. Their success highlights the importance of national team cohesion and tactical acumen
  • Italy and Portugal: Both nations, with rich football histories, remained in the top 10 despite facing challenges. Italy, despite missing out on the 2022 World Cup, showed quality in European competitions. Portugal, with a mix of experienced and emerging talents, continued to be a formidable side.
  • Argentina and France's Prominence: Argentina and France followed Brazil in the rankings, holding the 2nd and 3rd spots, respectively. Argentina's high ranking was partly due to their Copa America victory in 2021 and their performances leading up to the 2022 World Cup. France, the 2018 World Cup winners, continued to showcase their depth of talent and competitiveness on the international stage.
